# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, same underlying need:

- **Content creators** with an engaged audience (5K–500K followers) who know they should monetize with a digital product (course, e-book, video program) but have no time or production skill to build it themselves.
- **Amazon sellers** who need professional motion-design product videos and listing assets to compete, but don't have a camera crew or in-house video team.

Both are visiting the site cold, from an ad, DM, or referral — they've never heard of the company before. They are busy, skeptical of "agency" pitches, and evaluating in seconds whether this looks like a serious operator or another generic marketing service.

## Product Purpose

LaunchYourProduct (LYP) is a done-for-you build partner: it builds and launches a creator's first (or next) digital product — or produces AI-accelerated motion-design video ads for Amazon sellers — in weeks, not months, using AI-accelerated production (Claude for scripts/copy, Higgsfield for motion video) curated by the founders. Success is the visitor booking the free monetization audit call because the page made them think "these guys clearly know what they're doing."

## Brand Personality

Premium, calm, confident, effortless — closer to a category-defining software product (Apple, Linear, Stripe, Framer) than a marketing agency. Quiet competence over hype; craft as the trust signal, not adjectives.

**References:** Apple (restraint, product-first framing), Linear (dark, precise, minimal chrome), Stripe (density-appropriate technical credibility), Framer (motion as proof of craft, not decoration).

## Anti-references

- Generic "growth agency" language and stock marketing-speak ("synergy", "unlock your potential", "game-changing").
- Interchangeable, unverifiable testimonial cards (initials-in-a-circle, no real specificity) — the site is new and must lead with demonstrated competence (portfolio, case studies, before/after work) rather than claimed social proof.
- SaaS-cliché visual scaffolding: gradient text, side-stripe accent borders, uppercase tracked eyebrows on every section, numbered 01/02/03 markers as decoration rather than a real sequence.

## Design Principles

- **Demonstrate, don't claim.** Every expertise claim needs a visible artifact next to it (mockup, screenshot, before/after, real number) — proof of competence replaces client-count social proof while the company is new.
- **One question per section, one path forward.** Each section resolves a specific visitor doubt (Do I need a big audience? How much time does this take? Does AI mean lower quality?) and hands off cleanly to the next.
- **Restraint reads as premium.** Calm density, generous whitespace, and precise motion signal more competence than loud claims or busy layouts.
- **Distinct CTAs, not a repeated button.** Wording should match the commitment level and position in the page, never the same label copy-pasted down the page.
- **Motion is part of the craft argument.** Micro-interactions and reveals should feel intentional and Apple/Linear-grade, since the site is selling execution quality — sloppy motion undercuts the pitch.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large/bold text ≥3:1, full keyboard operability, visible focus states, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation (crossfade or instant, no motion suppression of content visibility).
